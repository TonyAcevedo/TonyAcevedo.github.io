Filter Job Aid

This job aid will help agents ensure a Member has properly installed filters on the DSL line. It is important for all telecommunication devices to have been filtered prior to issuing an ASI Magic ticket for No sync, Intermittent sync, BBT=RED, or OOS.

 

BBT and missing filters:

Incorrect hookup of filters to telecommunication devices (satellites, telephones, alarms, fax machines, etc) can affect the results or create a red or Out of Service condition (OOS) with the BBT. If the BBT is unable to sync with a modem, for any reason, the BBT will return in the status box of the line attributes section an Out of Service Condition (OOS). Verify that Member's filters are installed correctly, there is dial tone on the DSL WTN, the modem has power, and all cords/cables are installed properly.

 

Note: When BBT test has an Attenuation of Upstream higher than Downstream, the issue is most likely filters or inside wiring. Please refer to article #5870 How to Check Your Cables and Filters before forwarding to ASI.

Ensure the Member has installed filters on all telecommunication devices that are connected to the DSL phone line except for the DSL modem. *

    • If the DSL modem and another device share the same phone jack, the Member should determine the following:
      • If using a two-plug filter, the DSL cable should be plugged into the side of the two-plug filter labeled DSL/HPN. The phone, fax machine, analog modem, or other telecommunications device should be plugged into the side of the two-plug filter labeled phone.
      • If using a single plug filter, a two-to-one adapter is required. The adapter should be plugged into the phone jack with the single plug filter plugged into one side of the adapter and the DSL cable plugged into the other side of the adapter.
      • If using a wall mount filter, then it should be connected to the wall and the phone should be connected back on it.

Ensure the Member is using the modem power cord that was sent to them in the DSL kit.

*Devices such as fax machines, caller ID boxes, burglar alarms, satellite systems or any other telecommunications devices that are connected to a DSL phone line require a line filter which prevents these devices from disrupting the DSL signal on the phone line. Symptoms of poorly installed filters could include no sync or a slow connection.
